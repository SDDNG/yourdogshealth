Good Stimulation is Essential to Happiness

Physical exercise alone isn’t enough to ensure a healthy and happy dog, they need lots of stimulation. When you consider that many breeds of dogs were intended to do quite demanding jobs such as working in teams to guard isolated flocks or hunting in unison with their owners, it’s clear that asking a dog to sit alone and stare at a wall for hours a day is just asking for trouble. There are many games or tasks that can be given to your best friend e.g. having to find their food rather than just giving it to them in a bowl or doing obedience tasks, here are some ideas. One easy thing to do that will enrich your dog’s life is to give him time to stop frequently and smell their surroundings when on a lead walk, they will spend a lot of time scenting when off lead as this is the way they interact with the world. Another key thing to remember is that dogs are social animals, so exposing them to other dogs in an appropriate setting when they are young and maintaining this throughout their life is one of the best things you can do to have a happy dog. Finally, one great advantage to mentally stimulating a dog is that it tires them out and you may not need quite as much time to exercise.
