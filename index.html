What Our Dogs Need

Our dogs give us so many things, Rodney Habib and Dr Karen Shaw Baker writing in The Forever Dog, noted how they provide owners with a reason to get outside and exercise and give us unconditional love, the perfect antidote to a stressful world. Indeed, according to Habib and Baker, studies show that dogs can lower blood pressure, keep us active, reduce heart attack and stroke risk, boost our self-esteem, encourage social engagement, force us to be outdoors in nature, and trigger the release of potent chemicals in our bodies that make us feel safe, connected, and content. One study linked dog ownership to a reduction in all-cause mortality of 24% and we’ve also learned that dogs can help children develop stronger immune systems and soothe the stressors of adolescence. No wonder, the authors dubbed them “Humanity’s Best Friend”. Given all of this, surely, it’s our duty to provide for these perfect companions to the very best of our ability, to give them all they need to lead their best possible life. There are three essential elements to doing this, namely, exercise, diet and a fulfilling environment. Finally, if you don’t yet have a dog but are considering adding one to your family, this is a great place to start researching specific breeds.
