Dogs Love to Move

Unfortunately, just as the modern world conspires to make us less active and more sedentary, our dogs are suffering from the same malaise. More than half of our pets are overweight or obese and a key factor is a lack of exercise. Most dog breeds were originally bred to do demanding jobs, for example the Corgi, a dog many people see as a cute couch companion, used to herd cattle for their owners by darting between the much larger animals nipping at their heels and avoiding powerful retaliatory kicks from the hoofs. There were other dogs used for herding, hunting, drafting, guarding and personal protection. Given these origins most breeds have specific exercise requirements, according to dogtime.com most breeds need 30 to 60 minutes exercise a day. If you have a gun dog such as a Springer Spaniel or Weimaraner, you could be looking at 2 hours daily and for most breeds some of this time should include off-lead time as well, in fact off lead walks where the dog can regulate his own exercise may often be preferable. There are other forms of exercise too such as drafting, swimming and dog sports such as agility, this page from the Amercian Kennel Club is a great starting point for ideas.
